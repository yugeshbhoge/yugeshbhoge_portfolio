Background & Motivation

My academic journey has been a transformation from understanding the nuts and bolts of terrestrial machines to exploring the supreme mechanics of the cosmos. Starting in Mechanical Engineering, I developed a rigorous foundation in fluid dynamics, simulation, and design optimization. However, I realized that the universe itself is the ultimate laboratory for these principles.

My vision is to bridge these worlds.

I aim to apply robust computational techniques from finite volume methods to Bayesian inference pipelines, to decode the transient universe. Specifically, I am driven to solve the mysteries of compact binary mergers, using Gravitational Waves as the key to unlock the physics of extreme matter densities found in Neutron Stars.

Distinguishing BNS vs NSBH Mergers
Master's Thesis

Collaborators:
Ish Gupta, Mukul Bhattacharya, Rahul Kashyap

The Challenge: Gravitational wave signals from Binary Neutron Star (BNS) and Neutron Star-Black Hole (NSBH) mergers can look dangerously similar when the companion mass is low.

Methodology

Implemented a Bayesian pipeline utilizing the Arnett-Chatzoupoulos-Villar (ACV) semi-analytical model. By analyzing the "blue" ejecta component, we can break the degeneracy.

Constraining Equation of State
Bayesian Inference Project

Collaborators:
Vasudev Dubey, Ish Gupta, Rahul Kashyap

Neutron stars contain the densest matter in the universe. Their internal structure is described by the Equation of State (EoS). I used Bilby to recover tidal deformability from simulated NSBH signals.

Key Result

By calculating Bayes Factors for various models (SLy, APR4, DD2) against an injected signal (SLy), we successfully ruled out stiffer EoS models (like H4) with high confidence.

Rayleigh-Taylor Instability in stellar interiors
Fluid Dynamics (MHD)

Collaborators:
Vasudev Dubey, Lucky Chaudhary

Investigated the instability that occurs when a dense fluid sits atop a lighter fluid in stellar interiors.

  • Implemented 2D finite-volume simulation of compressible Euler equations.
  • Analyzed the stabilizing effect of magnetic fields (MHD) on short-wavelength perturbations.
